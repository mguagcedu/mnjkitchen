Barriers and Obstacles

Key obstacles that drive food insecurity and practical paths to reduce them.

Major Barriers

  • Poverty and inequality — Root drivers of hunger that compound through limited access to education, unstable employment, and constrained resources.
  • Economic shocks — Events like COVID-19 reduce incomes and disrupt supply chains. Investing in resilient, sustainable food systems helps communities prepare for future shocks.
  • Eligibility restrictions — Program rules for SNAP and food pantries can exclude people with the greatest need. Revisiting poverty guidelines and benefit amounts improves access.
  • Environmental degradation — Floods and droughts damage agriculture infrastructure and reduce yields. Regenerative farming strengthens soil and water quality and supports worker equity.
