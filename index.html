Barriers/Obstacles (add to Barriers page)

  • Poverty and Inequality — Root drivers of hunger that compound through poor education, unstable employment, and limited resources.
  • Economic Shocks — Events like COVID-19 reduce incomes and disrupt supply chains; investing in resilient, sustainable food systems helps communities withstand future shocks.
  • Eligibility Restrictions — Program rules for SNAP and food pantries can exclude those most in need; revisiting poverty guidelines and benefit levels can improve access.
  • Environmental Degradation — Floods, droughts, and degraded infrastructure reduce yields; regenerative farming improves soil, water, climate resilience, and farmworker equity.

Suggested copy for the Barriers page:

Poverty and inequality are the root causes of hunger in the world. Breaking the cycle of deprivation would be the ultimate solution; poor education, poor housing and living conditions, unemployment, and limited resources perpetuate food insecurity.

Economic shocks like COVID-19 increased food insecurity across many countries by reducing incomes and disrupting food supply chains. Investing in sustainable food systems can better prepare communities for future shocks.

Eligibility restrictions for programs such as SNAP and food pantries create significant issues for those who need support the most. Revising federal poverty guidelines and increasing benefit amounts would help address these gaps.

Environmental degradation is another driver of food insecurity. Floods and droughts damage agricultural infrastructure and reduce harvests. Regenerative agriculture prioritizes climate, ecosystems, water quality, people’s health, and equity for farmers and farmworkers.
